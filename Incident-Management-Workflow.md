## Objective
To restore normal IT service operations as quickly as possible while minimizing business impact and ensuring adherence to SLA commitments.

## Scope
This workflow applies to all IT incidents reported by enterprise users through calls, email, chat, or ITSM tools.

## Incident Definition
An incident is any unplanned interruption to an IT service or reduction in the quality of an IT service.

## Incident Lifecycle

### 1. Incident Logging
- Incident reported via phone, email, chat, or self-service portal
- Ticket created in ITSM tool with accurate details
- User, device, and service information captured

### 2. Incident Categorization & Prioritization
- Categorize incident based on service and issue type
- Assign priority based on impact and urgency
- SLA timelines applied according to priority level

### 3. Initial Diagnosis
- Perform basic troubleshooting and validation
- Check for known issues or existing incidents
- Attempt first-contact resolution where possible

### 4. Escalation
- Escalate to appropriate resolver group if not resolved
- Provide complete troubleshooting notes and findings
- Maintain ownership until resolution

### 5. Resolution & Recovery
- Apply fix or workaround to restore service
- Validate resolution with the user
- Monitor service stability if required

### 6. Incident Closure
- Update ticket with resolution details
- Confirm user acceptance
- Close incident as per ITSM process

## Communication
- Provide timely updates to users during the incident lifecycle
- Notify stakeholders in case of high-impact incidents

## Outcome
Ensure incidents are resolved efficiently with minimal disruption while maintaining SLA compliance and user satisfaction.
